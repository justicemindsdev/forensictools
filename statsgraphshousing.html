Illegal Evictions

Despite 8,748 cases of illegal eviction reported in 2022, only 26 landlords were convicted - just 0.3% of total cases.

What is Illegal Eviction?

Illegal eviction involves forcefully removing tenants from their homes without a court order or legal reason. It often involves:

  • Changing locks
  • Removing tenant possessions
  • Threatening behavior
  • Cutting off utilities
  • Physical removal

Labour MP Karen Buck compared the practice to domestic abuse, stating that "illegal eviction often takes place out of sight, involving violence. It is the most brutal of robberies."

Landlord Approaches to Smoking

Legal Regulation

  • Landlords can advertise for non-smoking tenants
  • Tenancy agreements can include no-smoking clauses
  • HMO shared areas are covered by Smoke Free Law of 2017
  • Insurance policies may be voided if set for non-smoking tenants

Smoking can devalue property as nicotine and chemicals build up in walls, floors, surfaces and furniture, in addition to being a major fire risk.

Recommended Approaches

  • Flag smoking issues with tenants directly
  • Verify insurance coverage
  • Potential for larger deposit to cover refurbishment
  • Rent adjustments after fixed term to compensate for damage

Attempting to evict specifically for smoking carries legal uncertainty and is not generally recommended as a first response.

Landlord Harassment

Landlord harassment is an ongoing, aggressive behavior carried out by landlords to pressure or intimidate tenants, often to vacate properties without formal eviction procedures.

What Constitutes Harassment?

  • Unnecessary rent increases
  • Deliberately failing to maintain the property
  • Refusing to accept rent payments
  • Cutting off utilities
  • Entering property without consent
  • Changing locks
  • Removing tenants' belongings
  • Withholding security deposits
  • Verbal or physical threats
  • Creating persistent nuisance

What is NOT Considered Harassment

  • Failing to repair non-essential items (e.g., washing machine)
  • Sending bill payments for tenant-caused damages
  • Yearly rent increases without property upgrades
  • Repairs or inspections with proper notice
  • Emergency repairs without consent
  • Installing security cameras
  • Contacting about overdue rent payments
  • Market-rate rent increases with proper notice
  • Eviction for non-payment of rent
  • Notifications about lease violations

Legal Steps for Tenants

Immediate Actions

  1. Document everything - Keep accurate records with dates of all incidents
  2. Gather evidence - Photos, videos, witness statements
  3. Emergency police contact - For immediate threats or illegal entry
  4. Seek legal advice - Contact housing solicitors or support organizations

Formal Procedures

  1. Lodge police complaint - Particularly for threats or illegal eviction
  2. Legal representation - Essential for gathering evidence and building a case
  3. Court petition - After professional assessment of case viability
  4. Council intervention - Local authorities have powers to address harassment

Renters Reform Bill

The Renters Reform Bill is currently making its way through Parliament, but recent amendments have prompted criticism from housing charities.

Original Promises

  • Complete abolition of Section 21 "no-fault" evictions
  • Introduction of open-ended tenancies
  • Stronger rights for tenants to challenge poor conditions
  • Landlord property portal to improve compliance
  • Strengthened possession grounds for landlords
  • Faster court processes for legitimate evictions

Recent Amendments and Concerns

  • Implementation of Section 21 ban could be indefinitely delayed
  • Reintroduction of some fixed-term tenancies
  • Potential weakening of tenant protections
  • Shelter and other organizations withdrew support after amendments
  • Calls for House of Lords to strengthen the draft legislation

Current and Proposed Solutions

Current Legal Framework

  • Housing Act 1988 - Established Section 21 evictions
  • Protection from Eviction Act 1977 - Makes illegal eviction a criminal offense
  • Housing and Planning Act 2016 - Introduced civil penalties up to £30,000
  • Smoke Free Law 2017 - Prohibits smoking in enclosed shared spaces
  • Homes (Fitness for Human Habitation) Act 2018 - Requires rental properties to be fit for habitation

Proposed Improvements

  • Complete ban on Section 21 evictions
  • Longer notice periods for tenants
  • Better council resources to enforce housing standards
  • Landlord registry and licensing schemes
  • Stronger penalties for illegal evictions and harassment
  • Housing courts to speed up legitimate cases
  • Uprating Housing Benefit in line with local rents

Polly Neate, CEO of Shelter: "With the Bill now in the hands of the Lords, peers of all stripes must overhaul this threadbare legislation and deliver the change that renters desperately need. Without serious amends to give tenants greater protection from eviction after moving in and longer notice periods, renters' best hope of a stable home will be lost."
